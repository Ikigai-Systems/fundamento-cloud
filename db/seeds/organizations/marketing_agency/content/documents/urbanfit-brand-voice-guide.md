# UrbanFit - Brand Voice Guide

## About UrbanFit

UrbanFit is an activewear brand for people who move through their city with purpose. Their customers are not just gym-goers -- they are runners, cyclists, yoga practitioners, and anyone who sees fitness as part of their daily life, not a separate activity. The brand sits at the intersection of performance and street style.

## Voice Attributes

### Energetic
The copy should feel alive and forward-moving. Short sentences. Active verbs. No passive voice where you can avoid it. Read it out loud -- if it sounds flat, rewrite it.

### Inclusive
UrbanFit is for every body and every fitness level. Avoid language that implies a single ideal. "Your best" always beats "the best." Never shame, gatekeep, or assume the audience's fitness journey.

### Motivating
Encourage without being preachy. We are a friend who shows up at 6am to run with you, not a drill sergeant. The tone is "you've got this" rather than "push harder."

### Real
Skip the jargon and marketing-speak. Write how people actually talk. If a sentence sounds like it belongs in a press release, cut it.

## Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Use "you" and "your" to speak directly to the reader
- Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
- Lead with benefits, not features
- Celebrate small wins ("3K this morning -- not bad for a Tuesday")
- Use contractions naturally (you're, it's, we're)

**Don't:**
- Use "no excuses" or "no pain no gain" language
- Reference body weight, size, or appearance as motivation
- Overuse exclamation marks (one per post maximum)
- Use fitness jargon without context ("AMRAP," "PR" -- spell it out)
- Make claims we cannot back up ("best workout ever")

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

**Instagram captions**: Keep it conversational and under 150 words. Lead with a hook in the first line. End with a clear CTA or question to drive engagement.

**Instagram Stories**: Punchy, one-liner overlays. Think bumper sticker energy. Always include a swipe-up or link sticker when promoting products.

**TikTok**: Script-free feel. Voiceover should sound unscripted even if it is planned. Match trending audio when appropriate, but never force it.

**LinkedIn**: More polished but still human. Focus on the brand's mission and community impact. This is where we share the "why" behind the product.

## Hashtag Strategy

**Always use**: #UrbanFit #MoveYourWay #CityStrong

**Rotate**: #UrbanFitCrew #StreetToStudio #FitForTheCity #MoveDaily

**Campaign-specific**: Create unique hashtags per campaign (e.g., #UrbanFitSummer26) and include them in the content brief.

Limit hashtags to 8-10 per Instagram post. For TikTok, use 3-5 relevant hashtags max.
