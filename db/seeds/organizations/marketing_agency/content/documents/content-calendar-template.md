# Content Calendar Template

## How to Use This Calendar

This is our central content calendar for scheduling and tracking all client social media posts. Every piece of content -- from Instagram stories to LinkedIn articles -- should be logged here before it goes live.

## Workflow

1. **Draft**: Author creates content and adds an entry with status "Draft"
2. **In Review**: Submit for review by tagging the account manager in a comment
3. **Approved**: Account manager or client approves the content
4. **Scheduled**: Elena queues it in the scheduling tool and updates the status
5. **Published**: Automatically updated once live, or manually marked after posting

## Best Practices

**Planning ahead**: Aim to have content planned at least two weeks in advance. Last-minute posts tend to underperform and create unnecessary stress for the team.

**Platform-specific formats**: Don't reuse the same content across platforms without adapting it. What works on Instagram (visual-first, short captions) won't work on LinkedIn (longer thought-leadership copy). Tailor the message to each channel.

**Content mix**: Follow the 70-20-10 rule for each client:

- **70%** value-driven content (tips, education, entertainment)
- **20%** shared or curated content (UGC, industry news)
- **10%** promotional content (product launches, sales, CTAs)

**Timing**: Use platform analytics to post during peak engagement windows. As a general guideline:

| Platform | Best Times |
|----------|-----------|
| Instagram | Tue-Fri, 10am-2pm |
| TikTok | Mon-Wed, 6pm-9pm |
| LinkedIn | Tue-Thu, 8am-10am |
| Facebook | Wed-Fri, 1pm-4pm |

**Visual consistency**: Always pull from the client's approved asset library. If new visuals are needed, submit a request to Marcus or Alex at least three business days before the publish date.

## Naming Convention

When uploading assets, use this format:

`[client]-[platform]-[content-type]-[date].[ext]`

Example: `greenleaf-instagram-carousel-20260215.png`
