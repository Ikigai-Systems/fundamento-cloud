As you have created a document or a table and you want to mention someone or something in order to have somebody involved in the creating document or just your intention is that you want somebody to see something, you can use "@" function.

You can both mention someone (and this person will receive a notification) or point out/mention another document in this Organization (that document will be linked here)

When someone has mentioned you in the document, you'll see a number of notifications (1, 2, etc.) on the icon on the top of the document page.

![Notification2.jpg](attachment:452)

As a standard, this Notification icon looks like that:

![Notifications button.jpg](attachment:453)

You can also point out/mention any other document within your Organization. This is very helpful as you want someone to see also connected somehow documents (or you do it just for yourself to have in mind these connections)

In this case you just use @ and dropdown list will appear

![335.png](attachment:454)

and you just choose a document you would like to mention/inlude in current document.

In Fundamento list of contents is called a "Structure", since in fact it is the structure of your document and you are able to create it in a planned way.

Any time when you click "Turn into" (as you click six dots symbol on the blank field on the left side of the paragraph) and choose "Heading1", "Heading2", "Heading3", this will be reflected in the structure of your document since "Heading" is a part of the list of content.

![Headings.jpg](attachment:455)

And reversedly - as some part of the text is already a header (and appears in the Structure of the document) and you don't want it to be in the list of content, you just change a "Heading' for a "Paragraph"

![Paragraph.jpg](attachment:456)

and then part of the text will be no more part of the list of contents.

You may also use a key combination (shorcuts) to achieve simillar efect:

*   `ctrl` + `alt` + `1` turns block into H1 heading.
*   `ctrl` + `alt` + `2` turns block into H2 sub-heading.
*   `ctrl` + `alt` + `3` turns block into H3 sub-heading.
*   `ctrl` + `alt` + `0` turns block into plain paragraph
